Dafni Karavola

Data Journalism • Visual Investigations

Dafni is a freelance data journalist and visual investigator based in Athens, with a background in architectural engineering. She is a member of Reporters United independent investigative network reporting on Romani rights, state and corporate accountability and environmental issues using data analysis, cartography and OSINT techniques. She has participated in cross-border investigations and worked with Magma Magazine, Forensic Architecture/Forensis, Arena for Journalism in Europe and others. She was trained in data journalism at Columbia University in New York, Reasearch Architecture at Goldsmiths University, in London and architecture at the National Technical University in Athens. Her interests lie at the intersection of journalism and spatial practices and she loves maps.

Digital Evidence Lab

'When was this photo really taken?' 'Does this location match the official story?' 'How has this landscape changed over time?' As journalists, we face these questions daily. But in today's digital world, answers often lie hidden in plain sight - in social media footage, satellite imagery, and countless other visual and spatial traces. While these materials traditionally served as illustrations for stories, they're now powerful sources of evidence themselves. Through mapping, OSINT techniques, and data analysis, digital breadcrumbs can be turned into investigative breakthroughs, transforming questions into revelations.
